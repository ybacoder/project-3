Predict Your Future

Answer the following 31 questions to predict your high school success.

1. What is your sex?


2. What is your race/ethnicity?


3. Is English your native language?


4. Did at least one of your parents graduate high school?


5. Which category does your total family income in the past year fall into? (If you are not sure about the amount, please estimate)


6. Is your school located in a rural area?


7. What region of the United States is your school located?


8. Calculate your number of academic risk factors by reviewing the statements below and totaling how many statements apply to you.

  • Comes from a single-parent household.
  • Has two parents without a high school diploma.
  • Has a sibling who has dropped out of school.
  • Has changed schools two or more times (excluding changes due to promotions.
  • Has repeated at least one grade.
  • Comes from a household with an income below the federal threshold for poverty.
    Households are considered impoverished if family income is $20,000 or less and household size is 5 or less; $25,000 or less if household size is 6 or 7; $35,000 or less if household size is 8; and $50,000 or less if household size is 9 or more.


9. Overall, about how much time do you spend on homework each week while at school?


10. Overall, about how much time do you spend on homework each week outside of school?


11. Did you work during the school year?


12. In a typical week, how much time do you spend on school-sponsored extracurricular activities (for example, sports, school clubs)?


13. How much additional reading do you do each week on your own outside of school - not in connection with schoolwork? (Do not count any school-assigned reading.)


14. On average, how many hours per weekday do you spend watching TV/videos/playing video games?


15. How many hours a day do you usually use a computer other than for schoolwork?


16. How often do you spend time volunteering or performing community service outside of school?


17. How much do you agree or disagree with the following statement about your current school and teachers?
The teaching is good.


18. How much do agree or disagree with the following statement?
Because doing mathematics is fun, I wouldn't want to give it up.


19. How much do you agree or disagree with the following statement about your current school and teachers?
Other students often disrupt class.


20. Have you been recognized for good grades, for example, honor roll?


21. How important are good grades to you?


22. How much do you agree or disagree with the following statement about why you go to school?
I go to school because my parents expect me to succeed.


23. Among your close friends, how important is it to them that they get good grades?


24. How often do you come to class without a pencil/pen or paper?


25. How much do you agree or disagree with the following statement about your current school and teachers?
I don't feel safe at this school.


26. How many times were you absent from school during the first semester or term of this school year?


27. How many times did you get in trouble for not following school rules during the first semester or term of this school year?


28. How important is getting away from this area of the country to you in your life?


29. Does your family have access to the internet at your home?


30. Do you have your own room at your home?


31. How often do your parents check whether you have done your homework?
